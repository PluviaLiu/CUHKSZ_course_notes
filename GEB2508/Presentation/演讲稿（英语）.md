The right piece, however, is **too perfect and too still**. It’s designed to be comfortable and easy to look at, like a product on a shelf. It lacks the "creative friction" that makes art feel human.

And here is what I concluded: Yoneyama was trying to capture that feeling of discovery—the moment when everything you thought you knew is replaced by awe. But AI was just trying to deliver an economically successful product.

It came to me that, even if AI can mimic vibrant colors, it will **never** understand the **struggle behind a single line**, it cannot understand that sudden moment our hearts skip a beat because we’ve finally discovered something new.

**Let me be clear: I am not against AI.** But the problem is how we are currently using it. We design it to **grab attention** and **cater to simple desires**. But true art is not a machine that takes input and produces output. Drawing is a conscious act; it is a human way for artists to respond to the world they live in. What AI follows is the cold logic of **efficiency**. It takes the soul of our creative heritage and turns it into nothing more than data for instant consumption. This is a direct assault on the true spirit of art.

And what's worse, many artists are facing **serious violations of their rights**.Today, anyone can type that artist’s name into a prompt, and AI makes thousands of copies in seconds. **The artist is forced to compete with a machine that stole their identity, while big platforms make money from art they never paid for.**

Tech giants treat the internet like a **free market with no rules**. Everything we post is swallowed by their machines—**without permission, and without pay.** Creators are  losing **control** over their own work, but many people **laugh at them**, saying they are just "against technology." But this isn't about being scared; it's about **unfairness**. This is a **serious crisis** for every creator, and we can’t ignore it anymore.

**So, in all, where do I stand on AI?**

**Think back to photography.** It ended the era of classical realism, but it gave birth to modern art. **History is repeating itself.** Today, I believe that repetitive, low-creativity production **will** be replaced by AI. But I also believe that in the right hands, AI **can** eventually create something truly great.

**However, we need new rules.** We need a **better protection system**. When we publish a work, there should be a clear way to protect our rights. It’s about making sure internet communication is a **respectful conversation**, not just one-sided exploitation. Only then can we truly **protect the dignity of every creator.**

Why? Because a diary is meant to carry **your** feelings and **your** life.

When we see art—even with its "imperfect" brushstrokes—we are seeing **real emotions**. Let’s learn to appreciate that. Let's feel the value of the moment it was born, the feelings behind it, and its unique way of expression. Because **that** is exactly how we experience art.

**When art is truly seen, the aura of humanity lives on.**
