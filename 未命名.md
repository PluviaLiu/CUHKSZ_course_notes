## Title: SE-FlexFlow: A Hierarchical Design Framework for Non-Rigid Porous Materials via Reinforcement Learning Feedback

### 1 Introduction

- **Background**: High-performance porous materials with hierarchical structures are increasingly essential in fields like soft robotics and aerospace. Unlike traditional rigid structures, non-rigid (flexible) porous materials offer unique energy absorption and adaptability. However, designing these materials manually is challenging due to the immense design space and complex non-linear mechanical responses.
    
- **Literature Survey**: Existing works often focus on periodic minimal surfaces or simple lattice structures using topology optimization [1]. Recent advances have introduced deep learning for fast property prediction, yet most models struggle with the multi-scale complexity of hierarchical designs and the dynamic feedback required for non-rigid deformation [2].
    

### 2 Problem Statement and Formulation

1. **Problem Statement**: The goal is to develop an automated framework that can design non-rigid porous materials with specific mechanical properties by optimizing hierarchical structural parameters across multiple scales.
    
2. **Mathematical Formulation**: We define the design as a mapping $f: \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{P}$, where $\mathcal{D}$ is the hierarchical design space (comprising macro-scale topology and micro-scale unit cells) and $\mathcal{P}$ is the target physical property space. The optimization is formulated as a reinforcement learning (RL) problem:
    
    $$\max_{\theta} \mathbb{E}_{\tau \sim \pi_\theta} \left[ \sum R(s_t, a_t) \right]$$
    
    where the reward $R$ is defined by the negative $L_2$ norm between the simulated mechanical response of the non-rigid structure and the user-defined target properties.
    

### 3 Research Plan

- **Detailed Research Plan (3-Month Cycle)**:
    
    - **Month 1: Environment Setup & Data Preparation.** Finalize the literature review on non-rigid mechanics and set up the simulation environment (MuJoCo/DiffSDFSim). Construct a dataset of basic unit cell geometries.
        
    - **Month 2: Model Development & Training.** Implement the SE-FlexFlow hierarchical generator. Train the Reinforcement Learning (RL) agent to navigate the design space using feedback from the physics simulator.
        
    - **Month 3: Evaluation & Documentation.** Conduct comparative experiments against baseline topology optimization methods. Analyze the mechanical performance of generated samples and complete the final project report.
        
- **Methodology & Flowchart**: The project employs a "Design-Simulate-Feedback" loop. We use a hierarchical representation to decouple macro-shape from micro-porosity. A Reinforcement Learning agent acts as the "designer," receiving rewards from a non-rigid physics simulator (Finite Element Analysis or Particle-based methods) to iteratively refine the material distribution.
    
- **Proposed Methods**:
    
    - **Generative Models**: Variational Autoencoders (VAE) for latent space mapping of porous unit cells.
        
    - **Reinforcement Learning**: Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) for stable policy updates in high-dimensional spaces.
        
    - **Simulation**: Differential physics engines to provide gradient-aware feedback for non-rigid deformations.
